# Fresh Eyes Vision

Fresh Eyes is an investigative intelligence workspace for unsolved cases. The goal is not to replace detectives with AI. The goal is to structure chaotic case material so investigators can understand a case faster, see relationships more clearly, and identify source-backed investigative gaps.

## Product North Star

A detective inheriting a cold case should be able to say:

> “I understand this case better in 30 minutes than I did after weeks of reading files.”

Fresh Eyes should accomplish this by turning reports, interviews, evidence logs, lab reports, tips, photos, and other case material into a source-cited case graph.

## Core Product Principle

Fresh Eyes should never ask investigators to trust unsupported AI conclusions. It should show investigators exactly where to look.

Every important fact, node, edge, gap, and recommendation should link back to its source document, page, and quote whenever possible.

## Initial Wedge

The first meaningful product wedge is the **Cold Case Graph Builder**:

1. Ingest messy digital case files.
2. Extract people, locations, vehicles, evidence, statements, events, and relationships.
3. Resolve obvious aliases and duplicate references.
4. Build a timeline, relationship map, evidence matrix, and investigative gap list.
5. Keep every output source-cited and human-reviewable.

## Long-Term Vision

Fresh Eyes should become the operating layer for cold case review:

- Rapid case intake from boxes, scans, folders, and giant PDFs.
- Entity resolution across names, aliases, locations, vehicles, phones, documents, and evidence.
- Link analysis showing how people, places, vehicles, evidence, and events connect.
- Timeline analysis for victims, suspects, evidence, and investigative actions.
- Evidence intelligence that identifies what was collected, tested, unresolved, or worth retesting.
- Victimology maps centered on victims, relationships, routines, and pre-incident activity.
- Suspect timeline and alibi gap analysis.
- Durable AI agents that perform specialized, auditable processing tasks.
- Human review workflows for confirming, correcting, or rejecting extracted facts.
